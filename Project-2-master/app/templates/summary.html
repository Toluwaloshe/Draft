Female Workforce Participation and GDP Growth Over Time

Female Workforce Participation and GDP Growth by Continent


Globally, workforce participation among women has increased substantially in the last 28 years. At a growth rate of 6.2%, female workforce participation steadily increased from 1990 to 2018. On the other hand, year-over-year GDP change is more volatile. There are sharp declines in GDP growth in the early 1990’s, the early 2000’s and 2009 due to domestic and global recessions and economic downturns.

Female workforce participation varies considerably across continents. Continents with more emerging economies (Asia, Africa, and South America) have lower participation rates; South America being the outlier as countries within the region have done a remarkable job of reducing the gender gap through the educational advancement of women. Today, the education gap from a gender perspective in many large South American countries is almost closed and women are working more as a result. Countries with lower female participation (Europe and Africa) have seen the least amount of GDP growth over time; Asia being the outlier here due to the rapid economic growth of China and other “Asian Tigers”. Increased capital investment and an increase in productivity in the late 1970’s led to the rapid growth of those countries, rendering them major players in the global economy.
